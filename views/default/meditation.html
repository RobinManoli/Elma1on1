Sádhaná

{{=T("Intuitional practice, or spiritual meditation, is called sádhaná in Sanskrit. Sádhaná is not only sitting meditation, but also includes human conduct, and contemplating the idea of everything being Consciousness-Energy in everyday life. Sádhaná is the core of Ánanda Márga.")}}
»{{=T("The effort to transform the unit identity into the Macrocosmic “I” is called sádhaná.")}}«
»{{=T("The goal of sádhaná is the all-round elevation of human beings.")}}«


Mantra

{{=T("A mantra is a word which one repeats during meditation to free all the fetters of mental and physical life. A proper mantra must have two meanings: It must be meaningful in the ordinary sense that the word has a meaning, and at the same time it must be supported by the proper acoustic root. The acoustic root is the sound an action creates, for example the vibration ha-ha-ha is the acoustic root of laughing. In other words, a mantra does not only have a liberating meaning, but it also is the sound (or vibration) of the liberating meaning.")}}


Kiirtana

{{=T("Kiirtana is a kind of devotional singing, and means to sing the Supreme Consciousnesses glory in a loud voice. When people do kiirtana out of deep love, a highly powerful spiritual vibration is created. Those who do kiirtana feel that vibration in their minds, hearts, and everywhere. They become virtually intoxicated with unbounded bliss. That bliss is attainable only by a person of devotion.")}}

{{=T("In Ánanda Márga, when we sing kiirtan, we sing the mantra")}} “Bábá Nám Kevalam”, {{=T("which means “I am taking the name of that Singular Entity”, the Singular entity being the Universal Consciousness-Energy. This is a universal mantra, which anyone can use, both in kiirtana and meditation.")}}
